# Materia Medica Americana

**An integrated analysis of three primary sources of indigenous American botanical medicine**

The first systematic cross-source analysis connecting the *Historia del Nuevo Mundo* (Cobo, 1653), the *Badianus Manuscript* (Martín de la Cruz / Juan de Badiano, 1552), and the *Florentine Codex* (Sahagún, 1577) with modern pharmacological cross-referencing.

## What This Is

This archive provides what none of these sources offers alone:

- **First English translation** of Cobo's botanical sections (Books IV–VI of *Historia del Nuevo Mundo*)
- **Fresh translation** of the Badianus Manuscript from Latin, independent of the copyrighted 1940 Emmart translation
- **Integration** with the Getty Research Institute's Digital Florentine Codex
- **Convergence Analysis** — a unified plant database scoring each plant by the number of independent traditions that validate the same use
- **Priority Investigation List** — plants ranked by pharmacological research urgency

## The Founding Thesis

In 1971, Tu Youyou read a 4th-century Daoist medical text and found artemisinin. She won the Nobel Prize in 2015. The pharmacological knowledge documented in these three manuscripts — largely inaccessible due to language barriers and institutional neglect — may contain discoveries of comparable significance.
